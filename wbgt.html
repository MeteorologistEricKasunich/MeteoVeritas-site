Wet Bulb Globe Temperature (WBGT)

What is WBGT?
WBGT stands for Wet Bulb Globe Temperature — a measure that combines temperature, humidity, wind, and solar radiation to assess heat stress risk. It's more accurate than the heat index for evaluating safety during outdoor activity and labor.

We apply the Liljegren method, widely regarded as the most accurate open-source WBGT approximation. It includes:

  • Black globe temperature estimation
  • Adjustments for wind and solar radiation
  • Time-of-day and sun angle effects
WBGT Risk Levels:
  • Low: WBGT below 82°F — Minimal risk
  • Moderate: WBGT 82°F to 87.9°F — Use caution, hydrate
  • High: WBGT 88°F+ — High risk of heat illness

WBGT data is estimated using Open-Meteo weather model inputs and calculated via the Liljegren approximation.
